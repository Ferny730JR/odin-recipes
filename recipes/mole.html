Mole

Description

Mole is a very yummy Mexican dish. To quote Wikipedia: "Mole, meaning 'sauce', is a traditional sauce and marinade originally used in Mexican cuisine. In contemporary Mexico the term is used for a number of sauces".

Ingredients

  • 1 jar (8.25 oz) Doña Maria Mole Sauce
  • 4-6 pieces of chicken breasts
  • 4 cups chicken broth (or water)
  • 1 tbsp peanut butter (optional, for added creaminess)
  • 1-2 tbsp sugar (optional, to balance flavors)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Warm tortillas (for serving)
  • Optional toppings: sesame seeds, chopped cilantro, or sliced onions

Steps

  1. In a large pot, add the chicken pieces and cover them with water or broth. Add a pinch of salt, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to a simmer and cook until the chicken is fully cooked (about 20-30 minutes)
  2. Once cooked, remove the chicken from the pot and set aside. Reserve the broth for the mole sauce
  3. In a medium-sized saucepan, heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil over medium heat
  4. Scoop the Doña Maria Mole Sauce into the pan and stir continuously to soften the paste (about 2-3 minutes)
  5. Gradually add 3-4 cups of the reserved chicken broth, stirring constantly to dissolve the mole paste. The consistency should be thick but pourable; adjust with more broth or water as needed
  6. If you like, mix in 1 tablespoon of peanut butter and 1-2 tablespoons of sugar to enhance the sauce's creaminess and sweetness
  7. Simmer the mole sauce for 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking
  8. Add the cooked chicken pieces to the mole sauce, ensuring they are fully coated. Simmer together for another 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld
  9. Serve the chicken mole with warm tortillas and sides like rice or beans. Garnish with sesame seeds, chopped cilantro, or sliced onions if desired
